Example calculation of metrics:

  • Suppose your renovation budget is 125K and you notice three properties on the MLS: property A is listed at 300K, property B is listed at 375K and property C is listed at 400K.
  • Assume all are without your budget for purchasing a home. The model predicts a resale value for all three of 500K.
  • Therefore, potential gain for A, B and C are 200K, 125K, and 100K, respectively.
  • In this case, property A (black) would be classified as a safe flip because $200K is greater than what you will presumably spend on your reno ($125K) + model error ($20K).
  • Property B is classified as a risky flip (gray) because you would basically just make your money back, and C (red) is a property to stay away from because the model predicts that you wouldn't make your money back.
ROI is calculated as the percentage gain from your total investment. In the case of Property A, total investment would be $300K + $125K = $425K. If resale is 500K, then ROI is = $75/$425 = +0.17%.
